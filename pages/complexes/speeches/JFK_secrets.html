April 27th, 1961

The newly elected President John F. Kennedy gave an address to the American Newspaper Publishers Association, where he emphasized the importance of transparency within a free society and shared the same concerns his predecessor had, being the importance of balance between national security and the threats to it. Kennedy specifically addresses the threats that government secrecy can cause eventually leading to erosion of public trust. He called for a change of outlook by those who would act against the progress of America’s supreme goals and a reconsideration of governmental mission tactics.

He asked those who stand for the principles of this nation, to band together in recognition of a large global threat of “...a monolithic and ruthless conspiracy that relies primarily on covert means for expanding its sphere of influence.” Kennedy, recognizing and addressing the overwhelming threat imposed by secret societies in his speech states “It is a system which has conscripted vast human and material resources into the building of a tightly knit, highly efficient machine that combines military, diplomatic, intelligence, economic, scientific and political operations. Its preparations are concealed, not published. Its mistakes are buried, not headlined. Its dissenters are silenced, not praised. No expenditure is questioned, no rumor is printed, no secret is revealed.”

The second thing Kennedy asks of the audience is “...to inform and alert the American people. To make certain that they possess all the facts they need, and understand them as well. The perils, the prospects, the purposes of our program and the choices that we face.” He says “No president should fear public scrutiny of his program. For from that scrutiny comes understanding; and from that understanding comes support or opposition. And both are necessary.” Concluding the speech JFK remarks “Now the links between the nations first forged by the compass have made us all citizens of the world, the hopes and threats of one becoming the hopes and threats of us all.”
